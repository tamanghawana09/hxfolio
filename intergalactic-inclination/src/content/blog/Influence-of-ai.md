# The Influence of AI: How the Internet Changed Faster Than Most People Realized

> **AI isn't coming anymore. It has already arrived. The question is no longer *if* AI will change your industry—it's whether you'll adapt before it changes your role.**

Just two or three years ago, if I wanted to build an advanced animation for a website, my workflow looked completely different.

I'd search on Google:

- *"GSAP horizontal scroll animation tutorial"*
- *"How to create Apple-style scrolling effects"*
- *"Website animation with JavaScript"*

The results?

Usually one or two blog articles and several YouTube videos ranging from **2 to 3 hours** long. I'd spend hours pausing, rewinding, debugging, and trying to understand why my implementation didn't work.

That was considered normal.

Today, the process couldn't be more different.

---

# My Workflow Then vs Now

## 2–3 Years Ago

- Search Google for multiple tutorials
- Open 15 browser tabs
- Watch several YouTube videos
- Read documentation
- Search Stack Overflow
- Copy snippets from GitHub
- Spend 5–8 hours building one animation

The learning process itself often took longer than the implementation.

---

## Today

Now my workflow looks something like this:

1. Describe the animation I want.
2. Let AI generate a starting implementation.
3. Modify it to fit my project.
4. Read the code.
5. Improve it.

Within **one hour**, I often have something production-ready.

The difference isn't that AI magically writes perfect code.

The difference is that AI removes the repetitive work and lets me spend more time understanding and improving.

---

# Even YouTube Has Changed

One thing I recently noticed surprised me.

The YouTube videos that appear for the same topics have become dramatically shorter.

Instead of:

- 2 hour tutorials
- 3 hour masterclasses

I now see:

- 8 minute explanations
- 10 minute walkthroughs
- 15 minute implementation videos

Why?

Because AI now handles much of the boilerplate.

Content creators no longer need to spend an hour explaining how to write repetitive code.

Instead, they focus on:

- architecture
- concepts
- optimization
- best practices
- real-world implementation

The internet itself is adapting.

Many people still think AI is "the future."

I disagree.

AI has already become part of our everyday digital experience.

You probably interact with AI dozens of times a day without even noticing.

Examples include:

- Search engines generating AI summaries
- AI coding assistants
- AI customer support
- AI-generated product descriptions
- AI-written documentation
- AI-generated images
- AI-powered video editing
- AI voice generation
- AI search results
- AI email writing
- AI translation
- AI recommendations

Even social media algorithms increasingly prioritize AI-generated and AI-assisted content.

The internet has quietly changed.

# The Biggest Misconception

One of the biggest misconceptions is:

> **"AI will replace developers."**

The reality is more nuanced.

AI replaces repetitive work.

Developers who know how to use AI become significantly more productive.

Instead of spending hours writing boilerplate, they can spend more time on:

- solving business problems
- improving user experience
- designing scalable systems
- learning new technologies

The same applies to designers, marketers, writers, and entrepreneurs.

---

# AI Doesn't Replace Thinking

This is where many people misunderstand AI.

AI gives answers.

It doesn't automatically give **good decisions**.

Knowing:

- what to ask
- what to verify
- what to improve
- what to reject

is becoming more valuable than memorizing syntax.

Critical thinking matters more than ever.

Instead of only learning tools, we should focus on developing skills that AI cannot easily replace.

These include:

## Problem Solving

Understanding real-world problems will always be valuable.

## Communication

## Creativity

---

## Verification

## Continuous Learning

The technology landscape is evolving faster than ever.

The people who continue learning will consistently stay ahead.

# My Perspective as a Developer

As someone building websites and applications, AI hasn't reduced my interest in programming.

It has increased it.

Instead of spending hours searching for syntax, I spend more time experimenting.

I've been able to explore:

- motion design
- interactive websites
- AI-assisted development
- automation
- better UI/UX
- faster prototyping

AI didn't remove the joy of building.

It removed much of the friction that used to slow me down.

---

# The Future Isn't AI vs Humans

The future isn't about humans competing against AI.

It's about humans who effectively use AI outperforming those who don't.

Just as calculators didn't eliminate mathematicians and Photoshop didn't eliminate designers, AI won't eliminate every profession.

But it will change how we work.

The earlier we embrace that change, the more opportunities we'll create for ourselves.

---

# Final Thoughts

The internet has quietly entered a new era.

Learning that once took weeks can now happen in days.

Tasks that required hours can often be completed in minutes.

That doesn't mean expertise is becoming less valuable.

It means the definition of expertise is changing.

The real advantage no longer comes from simply knowing *how* to do something.

It comes from understanding **why** you're doing it, knowing **when** to use AI, and having the judgment to improve what it produces.

AI isn't replacing curiosity.

It's amplifying it.

The question isn't whether AI will influence your career.

The question is whether you'll use it to build the next version of yourself.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Has AI replaced web developers?

No. AI accelerates development by handling repetitive tasks, but developers are still responsible for architecture, problem solving, debugging, security, and user experience.

### Is it still worth learning programming in the AI era?

Absolutely. Learning programming helps you understand how systems work, evaluate AI-generated code, and build reliable applications.

### How has AI changed learning?

AI reduces the time spent searching for information and allows learners to focus more on understanding concepts, experimenting, and solving real problems.

### What skills will remain valuable despite AI?

Critical thinking, communication, creativity, problem solving, adaptability, and the ability to verify AI-generated information will remain essential.
